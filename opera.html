ACT II
Same Scene. Night. Awning removed. Moonlight. CAPTAIN discovered singing on poop deck, and accompanying himself on a mandolin. LITTLE BUTTERCUP seated on quarterdeck, gazing sentimentally at him.
BUT.

How sweetly he carols forth his melody to the unconscious moon! Of whom is he thinking? Of some high-born beauty? It may be! Who is poor Little Buttercup that she should expect his glance to fall on one so lowly! And yet if he knew—if he only knew!

CAPT. (coming down). Ah! Little Buttercup, still on board? That is not quite right, little one. It would have been more respectable to have gone on shore at dusk. BUT, True, dear Captain—but the recollection of your sad pale face seemed to chain me to the ship. I would fain see you smile before I go. SIR JOSEPH. Captain Corcoran, I am much disappointed with your daughter. In fact, I don't think she will do. JOS. Oh! then your lordship is of opinion that married happiness is not inconsistent with discrepancy in rank? ALL. Let the air with joy be laden, Rend with songs the air above, For the union of a maiden With the man who owns her love! DICK. Kind Captain, I've important information, Sing hey, the kind commander that you are, About a certain intimate relation, Sing hey, the merry maiden and the tar. CREW. Now, hark at that, do! Though foes we could thump any, We are scarcely fit company For a lady like you!
